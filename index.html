Bring your own idea

If you can describe it or show me a picture of it, there's a good chance it can be modeled and printed. Custom parts are one-offs built from your reference — sent over as prototypes, not proven products.

01

Describe the idea

What is it, what does it need to do, and where does it mount? A few sentences is plenty — more detail means fewer back-and-forth emails.

02

Attach reference photos

Pictures to model from: the part you're replacing, the spot it fits, or a sketch. Attach the images straight to your email.

03

Give the largest dimension

For each photo, the single biggest measurement (length, width, or height) with units. That's what scales the model correctly.

Send your request

The form drops your idea straight into my inbox — no email app needed. Add your details and send.

Reference photos: the free form can't carry attachments, so once you send this I'll reply and you can attach your photos to that reply — or use “email directly” to attach them right away.

About custom prints: these are made-to-order prototypes modeled from your references. They haven't been load-tested, fit-tested, or proven over time, and final dimensions depend on the photos and measurements you provide. Treat them as a starting point — not a certified replacement part.
